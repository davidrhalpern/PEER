# PEER Design Principles

## Human-Centered by Design

PEER intentionally preserves a human-in-the-loop model as a foundational design choice.

While technology enables scale and coordination, legitimacy, trust, and accountability in
government remain human-centered. PEER therefore prioritizes human judgment, human connection,
and human responsibility over automated interpretation or decision-making.

Automation within PEER, where present, is assistive rather than authoritative. Its role is to
surface information, reduce friction, and enable connectionâ€”not to replace policy judgment,
direct action, or render compliance determinations.

This principle exists to:
- build trust across agencies and roles,
- preserve organizational authority and discretion,
- enable meaningful peer-to-peer relationships,
- and ensure adoption precedes optimization.

Advanced automation may be introduced incrementally and deliberately, but only in ways that
reinforce human agency rather than obscure or override it.
